Value × Cost-of-Failure Matrix
Each acceptance criterion is placed in one of four quadrants based on Value (how much does getting it right drive the product?) and Cost of failure (how much harm does a failure cause?). Drag criteria between quadrants as your thinking evolves.
Critical (high value, high cost) Product-defining capabilities. Most test cases, strictest review.
Core (high value, low cost) Expected capabilities users rely on. Occasional misses tolerable.
Guardrails (low value, high cost) Rarely triggered but must never fail. Safety, compliance, refusals.
Deprioritize (low value, low cost) Exploratory or rare. Light coverage; revisit if usage grows.
